# Research

How can generative models produce solutions that are not only high-fidelity, but **provably feasible** with respect to hard physical, safety, and operational constraints - reliably enough to be deployed in real engineering and scientific pipelines? My research answers this question by treating constraints as **first-class design principles** rather than post-hoc corrections. I develop constrained generative models - spanning diffusion, flow matching, and neuro-symbolic architectures - in which geometric, physical, and safety requirements are embedded directly into training and inference. My research efforts are distributed among the following directions:

**Constrained Generative Models.** I build generative architectures whose outputs are **provably guaranteed** to satisfy hard physical, geometric, and safety constraints by construction - minimizing the gap between generated proposals and real-world feasibility.

**Decision-Focused Learning & End-to-End Optimization.** I develop learning-to-optimize frameworks that train models to produce **directly executable decisions** under uncertainty, integrating constrained optimization solvers end-to-end so that prediction and feasible action are learned jointly.

**Responsible Generative AI for High-Stakes Systems.** I investigate how provable constraint satisfaction, privacy, and equity guarantees can be co-embedded into a single generative pipeline - ensuring that AI-driven design and control in critical domains is **safe, fair, and auditable** from the ground up.
