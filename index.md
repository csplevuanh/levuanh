# About Me

I'm an incoming Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, advised by [Prof. Ferdinando Fioretto](https://nandofioretto.github.io/) as part of the **Responsible AI for Science and Engineering (RAISE)** group. I'm honored to be fully supported by the UVA Computer Science Scholar Fellowship. I hold a B.Sc. in Mathematics from Beloit College.

My research focuses on three interconnected directions: **Constrained Generative AI** - building models that respect geometric, physical, and structural constraints; **Responsible AI** - ensuring fairness, robustness, and transparency in generative systems; and **AI for Mathematics** - using generative models for mathematical reasoning, theorem proving, and verifiable discovery. Together, these directions aim to move beyond purely statistical reasoning toward outputs that are consistent, traceable, and reliable.

I'm also interested in collaborative and open‑sourced AI research, and I’m always happy to connect with others working on related problems.

## Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of constrained generative modeling, responsible AI, and AI for mathematics. I develop methods that embed constraints directly into the generative process (through latent space design, decoding mechanisms, or optimization) while also addressing fairness, robustness, and interpretability. A key application area is mathematical reasoning and discovery, where I analyze how generative models can be deployed to produce verifiably correct solutions, assist in theorem proving, and accelerate mathematical research while maintaining alignment with formal specifications.

1. **Constrained Generative AI**: Enforcing geometric (e.g., symmetry, invariance) and physical constraints during training or inference of generative models (diffusion, flow matching, LLMs). This includes designing latent spaces and decoding mechanisms that guarantee constraint satisfaction, as well as efficient training and post‑training methods (including RL‑based fine‑tuning) that reduce inconsistency and computational cost.

2. **Responsible AI**: Guaranteeing that constrained generative models are fair, robust, and transparent. I explore how explicit constraints can improve model reliability, mitigate biases, and make outputs more interpretable and traceable, particularly in high‑stakes science and engineering settings.

3. **AI for Math**: Developing generative models that assist in mathematical problem‑solving, conjecture generation, and theorem proving with formal verification. This includes integrating neural methods with proof assistants (Lean, Isabelle, Coq), enforcing logical and algebraic constraints during generation, and building systems that produce human‑verifiable or machine‑verifiable mathematical outputs.
