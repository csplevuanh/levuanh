# About Me

I'm an incoming Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, advised by [Prof. Ferdinando Fioretto](https://nandofioretto.github.io/) as part of the **Responsible AI for Science and Engineering (RAISE)** group. I'm honored to be fully supported by the UVA Computer Science Scholar Fellowship. I hold a B.Sc. in Mathematics from Beloit College.

My research focuses on three interconnected directions: **Constrained Generative AI** - building models that respect geometric, physical, and structural constraints; **Responsible AI** - ensuring fairness, robustness, and transparency in generative systems; and **AI4Science** - exploring the application of constrained AI to solve complex problems in science and engineering. Together, these directions aim to move beyond purely statistical reasoning toward outputs that are consistent, traceable, and physically reliable.

I'm also interested in collaborative and open‑sourced AI research, and I’m always happy to connect with others working on related problems.

## Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of constrained generative modeling, responsible AI, and AI4Science. I develop methods that embed constraints directly into the generative process (through latent space design, decoding mechanisms, or optimization) while also addressing fairness, robustness, and interpretability. A key application area is AI4Science, where I analyze how generative models can be deployed to model physical systems and engineering processes while strictly adhering to domain-specific laws and requirements.

1. **Constrained Generative AI**: Enforcing geometric (e.g., symmetry, invariance) and physical constraints during training or inference of generative models (diffusion, flow matching, LLMs). This includes designing latent spaces and decoding mechanisms that guarantee constraint satisfaction, as well as efficient training and post‑training methods (including RL‑based fine‑tuning) that reduce inconsistency and computational cost.

2. **Responsible AI**: Guaranteeing that constrained generative models are fair, robust, and transparent. I explore how explicit constraints can improve model reliability, mitigate biases, and make outputs more interpretable and traceable, particularly in high‑stakes science and engineering settings.

3. **AI4Science**: Exploring the application of constrained AI in scientific discovery and engineering design. This direction focuses on integrating physical laws (e.g., PDEs, conservation laws) and engineering requirements into AI architectures to ensure that generated solutions—ranging from molecular structures to power grid configurations—are feasible, valid, and scientifically grounded.
