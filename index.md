# About Me

I'm an incoming Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, advised by [Prof. Ferdinando Fioretto](https://nandofioretto.github.io/) as part of the **Responsible AI for Science and Engineering (RAISE)** group. I'm honored to be fully supported by the UVA Computer Science Scholar Fellowship. I hold a B.Sc. in Mathematics from Beloit College.

My research focuses on three connected domains: **Constrained Generative AI** - building models that respect geometric, physical, and structural constraints; **Responsible AI** – ensuring fairness, robustness, and transparency in generative systems; and **AI Safety** – developing methods to align generative models with human values, mitigate systemic risks, and enable verifiably safe deployment. Together, these directions aim to move beyond purely statistical reasoning toward systems that are correct, traceable, and aligned with safety specifications.

I’m also interested in collaborative and open‑sourced AI research, and I’m always happy to connect with others working on related problems.

## Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of constrained generative modeling, responsible AI, and AI safety. I develop methods that embed constraints directly into the generative process (through latent space design, decoding mechanisms, or optimization) while also addressing fairness, robustness, and interpretability. A key application area is **AI safety and alignment**, where I use constrained generation to prevent harmful behaviors, enforce ethical boundaries, and build systems that are auditable and resilient to misuse.

1. **Constrained Generative AI**: Enforcing geometric (e.g., symmetry, invariance), physical/logical, and regulatory constraints during training or inference of generative models (diffusion, flow matching, LLMs). This includes designing latent spaces and decoding mechanisms that guarantee constraint satisfaction, as well as efficient training and post‑training methods (including RL‑based fine‑tuning) that reduce inconsistency and computational cost.

2. **Responsible AI**: Guaranteeing that constrained generative models are fair, robust, and transparent. I explore how explicit constraints can improve model reliability, mitigate biases, and make outputs more interpretable and traceable, particularly in high‑stakes science and engineering settings.

3. **AI Safety**: Developing generative models and safety‑critical systems that operate within well‑defined boundaries. This direction focuses on using structural constraints (e.g., refusal to generate harmful content, adherence to formal safety specifications, monotonic risk bounds) to prevent catastrophic failures, enable human oversight, and support secure deployment. Applications include red‑teaming, adversarial robustness, value alignment, and runtime monitoring - all within a constrained generation framework that prioritizes **verifiable safety** over raw capability. My long‑term goal is to design safety systems that can be integrated into production AI pipelines, paving the way for technical leadership roles in AI safety engineering.
