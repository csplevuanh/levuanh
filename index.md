# About Me

I'm an incoming Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, advised by [Prof. Ferdinando Fioretto](https://nandofioretto.github.io/) as part of the **Responsible AI for Science and Engineering (RAISE)** group. I'm honored to be fully supported by the UVA Computer Science Scholar Fellowship. I hold a B.Sc. in Mathematics from Beloit College.

My research focuses on three interconnected directions: **Constrained Generative AI** - building models that respect geometric, physical, and structural constraints; **Responsible AI** - ensuring fairness, robustness, and transparency in generative systems; and **AI Safety and Societal Impacts** - addressing the long-term risks, ethical deployment, and broad human consequences of artificial intelligence. Together, these directions aim to move beyond purely statistical reasoning toward outputs that are consistent, traceable, and reliable.

I'm also interested in collaborative and open‑sourced AI research, and I’m always happy to connect with others working on related problems.

## Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of constrained generative modeling, responsible AI, and AI safety. I develop methods that embed constraints directly into the generative process (through latent space design, decoding mechanisms, or optimization) while also addressing fairness, robustness, and interpretability. A key application area is AI safety and societal impacts, where I analyze how generative models can be deployed securely and ethically to minimize systemic risks and align with human values.

1. **Constrained Generative AI**: Enforcing geometric (e.g., symmetry, invariance) and physical constraints during training or inference of generative models (diffusion, flow matching, LLMs). This includes designing latent spaces and decoding mechanisms that guarantee constraint satisfaction, as well as efficient training and post‑training methods (including RL‑based fine‑tuning) that reduce inconsistency and computational cost.

2. **Responsible AI**: Guaranteeing that constrained generative models are fair, robust, and transparent. I explore how explicit constraints can improve model reliability, mitigate biases, and make outputs more interpretable and traceable, particularly in high‑stakes science and engineering settings.

3. **AI Safety and Societal Impacts**: Investigating the broader implications of AI deployment, focusing on alignment, risk mitigation, and ethical safeguards. This direction explores how structural and fairness constraints can prevent harmful behaviors in AI systems and ensure their long-term impact on society remains beneficial and secure.

Why studying geometry is fundamental to the advances of multimodal AI? Let's consider this fun fact. In high-dimensional spaces, distances between points become nearly similar. This reshapes how similarity and structure should be interpreted in learned representations. This phenomenon, named Concentration of Measure, is important to data geometry in AGI because it explains why naive notions of distance fail. That's why it motivates the need for structured, constraint-aware representations that preserve meaningful variation.
