# About Me

I'm an incoming Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, advised by [Prof. Ferdinando Fioretto](https://nandofioretto.github.io/) as part of the **Responsible AI for Science and Engineering (RAISE)** group. I'm honored to be fully supported by the UVA Computer Science Scholar Fellowship. I hold a B.Sc. in Mathematics from Beloit College.

My research focuses on three connected domains: **Constrained Generative AI** - building models that respect geometric, physical, and structural constraints; **Responsible AI** - ensuring fairness, robustness, and transparency in generative systems; and **AI for Mathematics** - developing trustworthy math solvers that produce verifiable, logically consistent solutions. Together, these directions aim to move beyond purely statistical reasoning toward outputs that are correct, traceable, and reliable.

I'm also interested in collaborative and open‑sourced AI research, and I’m always happy to connect with others working on related problems.

## Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of constrained generative modeling, responsible AI, and AI for Mathematics. I develop methods that embed constraints directly into the generative process (through latent space design, decoding mechanisms, or optimization) while also addressing fairness, robustness, and interpretability. A key application area is mathematical reasoning and automated theorem proving, where I leverage constrained generation to build reliable math solvers that produce verifiable, logically consistent solutions.

1. **Constrained Generative AI**: Enforcing geometric (e.g., symmetry, invariance) and physical/logical constraints during training or inference of generative models (diffusion, flow matching, LLMs). This includes designing latent spaces and decoding mechanisms that guarantee constraint satisfaction, as well as efficient training and post‑training methods (including RL‑based fine‑tuning) that reduce inconsistency and computational cost.

2. **Responsible AI**: Guaranteeing that constrained generative models are fair, robust, and transparent. I explore how explicit constraints can improve model reliability, mitigate biases, and make outputs more interpretable and traceable, particularly in high‑stakes science and engineering settings.

3. **AI for Mathematics**: Developing generative models that act as trustworthy math solvers. This direction focuses on using structural constraints (e.g., logical consistency, algebraic invariance, step‑by‑step derivation rules) to ensure that generated mathematical solutions are correct, verifiable, and aligned with formal systems. Applications include automated theorem proving, symbolic equation solving, proof assistance, and error‑controlled numerical reasoning - all within a constrained generation framework that prioritizes precision over pure fluency.
