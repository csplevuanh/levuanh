# About Me

I'm an incoming Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, advised by [Prof. Ferdinando Fioretto](https://nandofioretto.github.io/) as part of the **Responsible AI for Science and Engineering (RAISE)** group. I'm honored to be fully supported by the UVA Computer Science Scholar Fellowship. I hold a B.Sc. in Mathematics from Beloit College.

My research focuses on three interconnected directions: **Constrained Generative AI** - building models that respect geometric, physical, and structural constraints; **Responsible AI** - ensuring fairness, robustness, and transparency in generative systems; and **AI for Math** - applying constrained AI to solve focused problems in mathematics. Together, these directions aim to move beyond purely statistical reasoning toward outputs that are logically sound, rigorous, and mathematically principled.

I'm also interested in collaborative and open‑sourced AI research, and I’m always happy to connect with others working on related problems.

## Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of constrained generative modeling, responsible AI, and AI for mathematics. I develop methods that embed constraints directly into the generative process (through latent space design, decoding mechanisms, or optimization) while also addressing fairness, robustness, and interpretability. A key application area is AI for Math, where I explore how generative models can assist in mathematical discovery and symbolic reasoning while strictly adhering to formal axioms and logical structures.

1. **Constrained Generative AI**: Enforcing geometric (e.g., symmetry, invariance) and physical constraints during training or inference of generative models (diffusion, flow matching, LLMs). This includes designing latent spaces and decoding mechanisms that guarantee constraint satisfaction, as well as efficient training and post‑training methods (including RL‑based fine‑tuning) that reduce inconsistency and computational cost.

2. **Responsible AI**: Guaranteeing that constrained generative models are fair, robust, and transparent. I explore how explicit constraints can improve model reliability, mitigate biases, and make outputs more interpretable and traceable, particularly in high‑stakes science and engineering settings.

3. **AI for Math**: Exploring the application of constrained AI in mathematical problem-solving and formal verification. This direction focuses on using generative models for automated theorem proving, symbolic regression, and discovering structural patterns in algebra and geometry. I want to ensure that AI-generated conjectures and proofs remain consistent with established mathematical traditions.
