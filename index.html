Mathematics meets Earth‑scale data. I build geometry‑aware learning systems that turn noisy multimodal signals—texts, graphs, satellite pixels—into actionable insight. My journey spans rigorous theory and deployable code: from spectral bounds for persistent Laplacians to millimetre‑level ground‑deformation maps. I am now seeking a Ph.D. home where I can push the frontier of geometric machine learning, applied topology/geometry, and remote‑sensing AI in one coherent research program.

1. Geometric Machine Learning

I design models whose inductive bias is written in curvature and symmetry. My ICML 2025 DIG‑BUGS paper introduces RN‑F, a contamination‑mitigation layer that treats noisy tokens as geometric outliers in embedding space. Ongoing work on spectral contraction for δ‑hyperbolic graphs shows how curvature can stabilise message passing, while Lipschitz bounds for persistent Laplacians guarantee robust feature evolution in dynamic complexes. I aim to extend these ideas to curvature‑controlled graph transformers and physically grounded generative models.

2. Applied Topological & Geometric Modeling

My theoretical toolkit—persistent homology, harmonic analysis, and functional calculus—guides data augmentation and surrogate‑model design. Works on Topology‑Preserving Scaling and Stability of Persistence Diagrams give provable invariance under non‑uniform deformations. In How Analysis Can Teach Us the Optimal Way to Design Neural Operators, I link operator spectra to network depth, informing TinyML compression without accuracy loss. Forthcoming projects explore geometric priors in structured PDE solvers and conformal‑invariant feature extractors for shape analysis.

3. Remote Sensing & Spatial AI

Earth observation demands algorithms that scale from kilometres to microns. My 2024 International Journal of Environmental Studies paper fused InSAR and geometric filtering to achieve millimetre‑precision subsidence maps for Ho Chi Minh City. At GeoHub.ai I architect physics‑informed pipelines that ingest optical, SAR, and LiDAR to monitor climate risks in real time. Current goals include self‑supervised representation learning for low‑resource SAR and geodesic‑aware fusion of multimodal satellite streams across ASEAN.
