About Me

Name: Le Vu Anh (Also go by Luffy)

Education: B.Sc., Mathematics, Beloit College

Research Interests:

  • Reasoning AI (Symbolic and Cognitive-Based Methods)

Affiliation:

  • Research Scientist @VCyber Lab, VinUniversity

I am currently a Research Scientist at the VCyber Lab at VinUniversity (remote). My work is dedicated to advancing reasoning AI systems that leverage symbolic reasoning and logic‐based methods to create robust and interpretable models. I work under the guidance of Prof. Tony Nguyen and collaborate with Prof. Sushmita Ruj at the University of New South Wales, and Prof. Lei Pan at Deakin University.

My bold ambition is to develop fully capable "thinking" AI systems that can help us solve the most challenging problems in science, engineering, and welfare. To achieve this objective, I strive to build models capable of symbolic reasoning and cognitive intuition, enabling them to autonomously diagnose errors and make well‐informed decisions. Throughout this journey, I have engaged in research on formal methods, algorithmic complexity in reasoning systems, and the integration of logic with learning techniques. My work has been featured in publications such as Physical Review Research, IEEE journals, and in certified patents. See other sections of my profile for more details.

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from Beloit College, where I built a foundation in algorithmic theory and mathematical analysis. I have strengthened these skills through research internships at Google Research, MIT, and the University of Tokyo.
