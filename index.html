I develop mathematical models to study the reliability and scalability of neural networks. Using tools from spectral theory, geometric analysis, topological data analysis, and graph theory, I aim to derive stability and robustness criteria and establish design principles that guide architecture choice and training in high-dimensional, multimodal settings.

1. Stability & Reliability

My work formalizes convergence and robustness conditions for learning algorithms. I identify invariants that ensure predictable performance under data perturbations, distributional shifts, and architectural changes.

2. Geometry & Topology of Information

I explore how geometric and topological structures such as Laplacian spectra, hyperbolic embeddings, and persistent homology can be used to characterize and design neural architectures that generalize reliably across scales and modalities.

3. Principled Design of Neural Architectures

By connecting mathematical criteria with practical architectures, I investigate how spectral contraction, Lipschitz constraints, and topological stability can serve as guiding principles for building scalable and trustworthy neural systems.
