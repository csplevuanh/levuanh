About Me

Identity: Vũ-Anh Lê (pronounced Voo-Ahn Leh)

Undergraduate Institution: Beloit College

Majors: Mathematics | Public Health

Research Interests:

  • Computational Modelling and Simulation
  • Autonomous Scientific Discovery

Current Appointments:

  • Research Assistant @MIT Nuclear Science and Engineering
  • Intern @MIT Summer Research Program
  • Collaborator @Vietnam's Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment

Have you ever wondered about the true spirit of liberal arts in our modern world, where everything has shifted towards industrialization after the division of labor? I've always sought opportunities to embrace this spirit, whether through scholastic endeavors, lab work, or reading - exposing myself to diverse topics to discover what the Creator has to offer.

During this quest, I was fortunate to discover in my sophomore year that I wanted to pursue a career as a computational scientist, using the data science pipeline to automate scientific discovery and incorporate scientific evidence into decision-making. I am deeply grateful to Prof. Haruko Wainwright at the Department of Nuclear Science and Engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology for guiding me in refining these skills through projects on environmental monitoring and revealing the behaviors of underground radioactive waste streams. Additionally, I have collaborated with Vietnam’s Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment on applying remote sensing technologies to track climate change impacts on hydrological behaviors.

As I look towards graduate school, I am excited to immerse myself further in computational modeling and simulation and to continue exploring my interests in earth and environmental science, as well as population health, wherever my skills are helpful.
