I am a Student Researcher (Research Intern) at Google.

My work lies at the intersection of the theory of machine learning and AI4science. I study how the core principles of logic, mathematics, and algorithms can build efficient, interpretable, and provable AI systems for real-world reasoning tasks and scientific inquiry.

  • Theoretical ML: Designing mathematically grounded models that unify logic, learning, and computation.
  • Reasoning Engines: Developing formal methods and solver architectures for explainable and scalable inference.
  • AI for Science: Using AI to accelerate scientific modeling, theorem discovery, and empirical understanding.

My academic path started with a Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) degree in Mathematics at Beloit College, followed by research internships at Google Research, MIT, and The University of Tokyo. These experiences shaped my long-term goal: to push the frontier of foundational AI systems.
