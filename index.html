I build technologies that help us model, reason, and design with structure. My research interest includes geometric machine learning, graph neural networks, and applied geometric modeling with a single goal: to develop versatile graph foundation models that can power scientific discovery and practical systems across domains. Whether through analyzing curvature in network dynamics or deploying geometric filters on satellite data, I connect deep structure to real-world use.

1. Geometric & Graph Machine Learning

I study how geometry and structure inform learning systems. Recent projects include δ-hyperbolic graph filters for hierarchical networks, spectral bounds for persistent Laplacians in dynamic filtrations, and geometry-aware techniques for mitigating data contamination in language models. I'm currently exploring how to embed topological priors into graph transformers and apply them to structured reasoning and simulation.

2. Applied Geometric Modeling

My work combines ideas from topology, harmonic analysis, and functional calculus to build structure-preserving tools. This includes topology-preserving augmentation, functional constraints for neural operator design, and spectral strategies for PDE surrogates. These methods apply to engineering systems, scientific computing, and any domain where form guides behavior.

3. Earth‑Scale Systems & Remote Sensing

I apply graph and geometric methods to large-scale sensing challenges. My work on InSAR-based land deformation monitoring and physics-informed image processing pipelines contributes to climate resilience and environmental intelligence. I collaborate with academic labs and startups to bring theory into real-world geospatial pipelines.
