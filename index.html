Current Appointments:

  • Research Assistant @MIT Nuclear Science and Engineering
  • Intern @MIT Summer Research Program
  • Collaborator @Vietnam's Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment

Vu-Anh Le is a rising senior at Beloit College, working towards a B.Sc. in Mathematics. With a keen interest in alternative energy sources, he intends to pursue graduate studies in energy science and engineering, specifically focusing on the development, integration, and commercialization of sustainable energy systems. As an MSRP intern, he works under the mentorship of Prof. Haruko M. Wainwright in the Department of Nuclear Science and Engineering, developing data-driven models for long-term monitoring strategies of radioactively contaminated Superfund sites. He is also a long-term collaborator with Vietnam's Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment, applying remote sensing techniques to reveal land deformation trends in Vietnam and the impact of hydroelectric power construction on these trends. These experiences have influenced him to pursue a career as an energy researcher, public interest attorney, and government consultant—accelerating the progress toward the United Nations’ SDG #7: Affordable and Clean Energy. In his leisure time, he participates in cross country, long-distance track and field, and reading human history. On this website, you can find either his CV or resume attached on the 2nd page.
