  • Former Research Intern @ Google Research & The University of Tokyo

I’m a Student Researcher in MIT’s Multisensory Intelligence Group. I explore how “sparsity inspires creativity”—the idea that true innovation often emerges when resources are strictly limited.

In the domain of tinyML, he is designing ultra-lean models and inference pipelines that thrive under tight compute, memory, and latency budgets—perfect for real-world and embedded applications.

These themes result in applications from scientific discovery—where compact AI accelerates experiments—to real-time robotics, where every millisecond and millijoule counts. Building on experiences at MIT, Google, and UTokyo, I aim to push AI toward systems that are not just powerful, but brilliantly efficient and creative under pressure.
